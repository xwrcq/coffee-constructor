About our history

A few years ago, the The Great Indian Coffee brand was little known to coffee lovers. Now this chain is considered the Ukrainian analogue of Starbucks - its points can be found on almost every corner of the capital and in more than 20 cities of Ukraine. The Great Indian Coffee representatives told RAU how the network works, what it is betting on, what difficulties it faces and what goals it sets for itself.
- The Great Indian Coffee is one of the largest coffee chains in the country. What is the secret?
- The secret is in the complex of correct decisions. First of all, it is the correct positioning, affordable prices and simple visualization. The Great Indian Coffee is a coffee shop that is always nearby. And of course, the product is the best coffee we have, equally good in the entire chain.
- How did the history of the network begin?
- Since the opening of the first coffee shop on March 8, 2014 in Kharkiv. They developed gradually - step by step. Before starting to sell the franchise, they had time to open coffee shops in various formats and regions. They decently "stuffed the cones", while it was possible to standardize all processes.
- How do you monitor compliance with standards? Is it possible to motivate the franchisee to adhere to the established service principles?
- Control over the implementation of standards rests with franchise managers and trainers. We also use an independent agency to monitor the quality of service. The main motivation for performance is profit, because when visitors receive a quality product in every sense (this is about coffee and about the coffee shop as a whole) - they want to come back again.
- What difficulties do you have to face on the Ukrainian market?
- The quality of the staff's work causes the greatest trouble. We invest a lot of effort and resources in the development of our employees, we create all the conditions for comfortable work with a salary worthy of our market. But finding good staff in Ukraine is not so easy. There are difficulties with finding quality premises, lack of culture in franchisees. Unfortunately, the mentality of Ukrainians is such that people prefer to invest money in "their own, but worse" than to buy a ready-made franchise, which in fact will bring more profit.
